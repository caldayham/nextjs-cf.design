Perry wanted a place for people to come together - treats for dogs and books for kids and adults.

The best part is he already trusted our quality from the last project and gave us lots of creative flexibility to bring this build to life!

—

- Extremely solid A-grad plywood core held together by 18 steel brackets and over 120 screws (some may say overkill, I say solid)

- Beautiful redwood exterior and floorboards for durability and aesthetics; sanded up to 320 grit and finished with teak oil for additional weathering and color protection.

- The pressure treated post goes over 2 feet into the ground with concrete sloped away from the base to prevent water pooling.

- The base is build with redwood 2x6’s and 2x4’s and another 6 steel brackets!

- The roof is layered redwood slats with a rougher finish and overhangs the front and sides by 4.5”

- All exterior finishing nails are stainless steel - not galvanized. A more expensive material but won’t stain the wood with iron-tannate streaks over time.

- The whole thing is completely sealed with wood glue and caulking making it rigid and waterproof.

—

With two levels (lower for kids and upper for adults) a easy push-to-open magnet system, and a treat box to boot there’s not many little libraries like this in Palo Alto, dare I say the Bay Area!
