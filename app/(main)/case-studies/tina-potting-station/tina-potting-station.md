For Tina's job, we started by building the base and the substructure of the potting station. Tina wanted a place for her to hang some tools and pot her plants outside in the sun, but she also wanted to make sure that the supplies didn't get super wet and she wanted a sturdy surface to work on.

We designed this potting station with a main flat bench area fully made out of redwood. There is a supported tool rack above it, which is basically a platform for pots or tools with hangers below it so that you can hang things like trowels and other small gardening utensils. On the bottom, there is a slatted rack which can be used for holding potting mix or storing large pots. Above, there is a corrugated stainless steel sheet roof which protects the entire thing from rain and also sun damage.

The whole thing is built from redwood, and redwood is nice because it has a natural ability to resist rot and to resist insect decay. The whole thing took us about two days. We started by building the base section and then proceeded to complete the roof area. Once we were done with the frame and the roof, we attached the sheet metal and the main surface area, and also the bottom slatted area. The whole thing was a really great experience, and this is one of our favorite builds because it really exemplifies the simplicity but also effectiveness of good carpentry. This is probably one of the best jobs that fits our business and our skills.
