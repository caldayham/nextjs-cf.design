Here’s how we built it:

-

First we took down the old deck that had been hosting local critters like possums, skunks, and rats and hauled it away to the reclamation center.

Then we built the substructure from pressure treated lumber set 16” on center with lots of blocking for great rigidity.

We set the main portion on nine concrete piles drilled 2 feet into the ground, six along the outside and three to support the center. 

Then we installed 19 gauge galvanized mesh to keep future critters out, and erected the awning substructure from redwood with a linseed oil finish. Redwood is great because it has natural tannins that stop insects and rot.

We used corrugated stainless sheeting for the awning roof to keep the area dry during the rain so our neighbor can read without worrying about the weather. The roof has a 7.5 degree tilt to keep sticks and leaves from building up.
