What makes a fence installation quality? 

First is safety, making sure there’s no utility or gas lines that could be damaged by the excavation process. We call 811 (USA North before-you-dig) to get a full report before we ever start digging.

Second is materials, redwood is amazing for its natural rot and insect resistance, but you also need to know when and where to use pressure treated lumber to really extend the lifetime of your fence.

Third is post depth, everyone’s felt a wobbly fence, it doesn’t lend itself to structural confidence and it’s a bit embarrassing if it’s yours. We excavate down 1/2 of the exposed post height, for this fence that is 2 feet into the ground for a 4 foot fence.

Forth is post level and plumb, before you think about setting the post you need to control for 4 axes of movement (up and down, left and right, forward and back, and the actual rotation of the pole along its vertical axis)

Without perfectly controlling for all these variables your fence will inevitably look crooked, or even have gate stability problems in the future.

Fifth is simple, setting the posts using enough concrete to stabilize and making sure you’re not creating a cup for water to pool around your post base and rot it out - simple but essential!

Sixth is attatching the kicker boards, rails, and pickets properly - fasteners are hidden behind trim, cuts are perpendicular and don’t warp the fence when secured in place or have overly visibke gaps, and a number of other small details.

Seventh is gate quality - keeping it square, stabilizing it properly so it can support weight across its length without sagging, attaching hinges and latches square and level so the gate shuts every time.

Eighth is a quality cleanup and excess material hauling to the proper disposal facility.
